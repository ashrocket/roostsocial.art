The idea

Roost is a carrier-pigeon messaging app (/Applications/Roost.app) where messages travel as birds in real time across a real map, and can carry six-color scratchpad drawings. roostsocial.art turns those drawings into a public gallery: send your art by bird to @ashrocket and a publish bot posts it to the site, tags it from your #hashtags, includes your message, and flies a bird back to you carrying a magic login link. Human art only — the tooling enables MS Paint-style control, not generative art.

The drawing pipeline (how Claude paints on Roost)

The scratchpad only accepts live mouse strokes, and its pen is speed-sensitive (fast = thick ~7px, slow = thin ~3px). So the workflow is: design artwork as normalized stroke lists → render in a pixel-true simulator (roost_sim/sim.py) → iterate until good → compile to timed synthetic mouse events (roost_sim/emit.py) → replay onto the real canvas. Today that produced a swallow-chasing-a-moth recreation of a reference photo (sent to Stacy), then bird self-portraits. The same idea, human-driven, became RoostPaint: tkinter editor with pencil/line/rect/ellipse/eraser/undo + a replay.py that paces strokes for thick/thin — downloadable from the site.
